Balloons EP Review

8/26/2019

0 Comments

 
Lighter than the air that surrounds it, a balloon in the sky embodies the free fleeting feeling of love with graceful nuance. Completely at the mercy of the wind currents, a balloon cannot control its height, nor its course. The more it soars, the graver the potential danger it faces should it fall to the earth. Cameron Payton’s latest project title, Balloons EP, is only fitting for an artist with a pseudonym like Nxmad, who presents, in merely four tracks, what occurs when a weightless wanderer is grounded.

    The EP opens with a lo-fi style that sets the tone for the whole project. “Listen (The Art of Being A Couple)” has the type of relaxed jazzy instrumental one might hear on a youtube Chill Hop channel on a rainy day. The simple yet catchy keys float back and forth over soothing kicks and claps, allowing space for the vocal sample of Theodore Zeldin’s Ted talk on how couples can have long loving relationships to carry the track. The speech sample is a common tactic in the growing lo-fi hip hop scene, and Nxmad’s splices are seamless in establishing the first step in the four song journey: listening.

    The second song, and the EP’s centerpiece, is a two part saga that starts off by continuing the jazzy feel of the previous track. Lively drums, soulful keys and a groovy bass line underlay a reversed sample that give the song a mesmerizing quality. The vocals sample spoken word poet Rudy Francisco’s performance of “If I were a Love Poet”, and serve as the portion of the journey where love is giddy, as it bubbles over in his words. When the poem finishes, the beat changes to an incredible breakdown in which the sample is slowed and crystalline synths and skittery high hats come in alongside bombastic drums. The only guest feature on the project, Txmorrow, sings an impassioned hook that asks a lover why they left. A moment that is equally heart wrenching as it is jaw dropping, the music feels like it is in slow motion, and it is a truly captivating moment.

    As the “Falling” beat fades and the waves of “Lost” wash in, pristine guitar strings hover in still air as the album reaches its emotional peak. Pieces of a shattering break up scene from Good Will Hunting portray the ugly side of love: the moment at which it is lost. This is the most compelling track on the EP, as the layers of guitars continue to tell the story long after the scene is cut for a beautiful outro.
​
    The titular and final track of the EP is a remix of Gabriel Garzón-Montano’s song “My Balloon.” As the singer who is known for being sampled on Drake’s track “The Jungle” sings about getting back on his balloon and going to the moon, Nxmad introduces lush strings to enhance the original. As the beat comes in, and Montano disappears, Nxmad himself recites a spoken word piece of his own about reconciling the difficulties of love. His words are heartfelt and well written, but it is clear Nxmad is more comfortable behind the boards and instruments as his delivery is somewhat timid: a juxtaposition to the conviction of the poem itself. As the compact, but moving journey comes to a close, it feels like watching a balloon float away and wondering where it will end up next.
