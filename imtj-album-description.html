Innerludes (More thAn JUst) Album Description

3/3/2019

0 Comments

 
“Cam, CJ and I don’t make music in genres, we make experiences.” Yxsterday’s reaction to his close friend and collaborator, Nxmad’s, recently released Balloons EP reads as more a reflection on their friendship (that is as organic as the art is) than a comment on the EP itself. Their music is a result of their experiences and they craft each of their tracks as such: a simultaneity that makes for a direct and personal connection of the sounds to their listener. Innerludes (More Than Just) takes poetry in a spoken and written form to new heights. It blends various inspirations, both musically and topically, to form a tight knit project that is as dynamic as it is cohesive. With Bon Iver and Fleet Foxes serving as his muses as much as Kanye West, Travis Scott, Chance the Rapper, Mac Miller, Ab-Soul and Kendrick Lamar, any attempt at classifications make this project a difficult shoe to fit. Yxsterday’s carefully spun tales weave multiple concepts over handmade in-house production into one breathing story that is anything but fabricated. Originally born from the idea that Spoken Word is typically relegated to Interludes in standard album formats, he decided to use this to his advantage: creating an entire tape of them. However, these poems aren’t just the spaces between songs, they are pieces of his inner psyche, his essence, his life and his soul. Truly expanding the bounds of his poetic talent, and the concept poetic license itself, he leaves the listener to get lost in the tape’s intricacies, and it’s all the more rewarding. Upon closer inspection, Yxsterday reveals in his music that there’s a bigger picture: it’s about more than just himself.
